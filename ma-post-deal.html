Post-deal Projects

Integration strategy

OTC Pharmaceutical and Consumer Goods - Russia

QUESTION

  • A British Pharmaceutical and Consumer Goods company needed to merge its operations in Russia with a recently acquired Russian FMCG company
  • Complexity: two incompatible business models (go-to-market, sales operations, logistics, incentive systems, and corporate culture), the risk of losing up to 50% revenue, time pressure due to previous unsuccessful attempts, very limited collaboration from the acquired company

RESULT

  • A hybrid business model developed respecting the core business principles of the acquiring company
  • An ambitious two-year integration and transformation plan including the digitalisation of field sales forces operations, the carve-out of non-strategic activities and the closure of non-strategic logistic capabilities

APPROACH

  • Description of both businesses including clients, products, operating models (e.g., sales KPIs, processes, organisation), capabilities and financials
  • Description of market perspectives and business priorities for each company stand-alone
  • Development of a merger strategy including vision, strategy, business, and operating model
  • Detailed financial modelling and simulation of various options
  • Multiple workshops with the board of management and the CEO
  • Very detailed description of the target merged company (financials, improvement levers and impacts, sales go-to-market strategy and operating model, logistic model, IT tools, incentive system, support function organisation…)
  • Detailed transition and implementation plan

CLIENT FEEDBACK

“You led the firm's biggest merger in the last 20 years, clarifying what, why, and how. Your support was crucial in our decision to go ahead with an ambitious integration plan, which exceeded our financial expectations.”

CEO, Global FMCG Company

Integration execution

OTC Pharmaceutical and Consumer Goods - Russia

QUESTION

  • A British pharmaceutical and consumer goods company required assistance in executing the integration of a recent acquisition. The challenge was to ensure a smooth process without impeding business results, given the tight integration deadline

RESULT

  • +60% revenue delivered in 3 years reaching £0.7Bn in the country at stake

APPROACH

  • Development of a detailed design for sales, marketing, logistics and support functions operations
  • Organisation of the integration team and capabilities including people and tools
  • Development of the program management (400 main actions and 11 workstreams), coordination and follow up
  • Support to each workgroup until completion
  • Development and implementation of pilots for the most complex operations before full-scale rollout
  • o Regular reporting to the board on the integration progress and its business impacts
  • Support to change management

CLIENT FEEDBACK

“Thank you for your hard work, relentless energy, the structure and discipline you brought. Without you, we wouldn’t have made it”

Head of Strategy – Global FMCG company

Carve out

UK Medical products company - Russia

QUESTION

  • A British global company producing and delivering FMCG, and medical products wanted to carve-out their medical business in Russia

RESULT

  • The carve-out was successfully executed with the carved-out company sold

APPROACH

  • Description of the company’s activities, products, clients, capabilities, and assets
  • Analyses to define the exit options including potential investors and buyers
  • Identification of shared capabilities and assets, and assessment of the level and nature of entanglement
  • Allocation of shared capabilities and assets
  • Evaluation of solutions for the carved-out activities to ensure continued operations
  • Calculation of de-synergies and impacts on the business, P&L, and balance sheet
  • Calculation of separation costs both ongoing and one-off
  • Identification and calculation of growth opportunities for the carved-out company to develop a compelling investment story
  • Development of an implementation plan
